- The canonical Forte Labs blog post introducing the PARA method, the organizational framework at the heart of [[Building a Second Brain]]. This is the definitive reference for understanding the four categories (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archive), how they relate to each other, and why organizing by actionability beats organizing by topic.

## Key Takeaways

> [!tip] The essential insights from this resource
> Distill the most important points. What would you want to remember if you came back to this in 6 months? Use progressive summarization, bold the critical phrases.

- **PARA organizes by actionability, not topic.** The question is "what is this useful for?" not "what category does this belong to?" This single shift eliminates most organizational ambiguity.
- **Only four categories needed.** Projects (active, with deadlines), Areas (ongoing, no deadline), Resources (reference, no responsibility), Archive (inactive from the other three). Everything fits.
- **Projects are the most important category.** They're the bridge between knowledge and action. A Second Brain without active Projects is just an archive.
- **The system is cross-platform.** PARA works in any tool - file systems, note apps, cloud storage, task managers. The structure is the constant; the tool is interchangeable.
- **Archive is not a trash can.** Archived items are preserved with their full context and links. They can be reactivated when they become relevant again.
- **Move things between categories as their status changes.** A completed Project moves to Archive. An Area that gains a deadline becomes a Project. The system is dynamic, not static.
